Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our platform, data, and methodology. If you have more questions, feel free to contact our team.

1. What is this platform?

This platform helps users discover, search, and analyze diplomatic speeches from major global actors, including the United States, China, and Russia. We aggregate publicly available speeches and provide tools for keyword analysis, trend visualization, and cross-country comparison.

2. Who is this platform for?

The platform is designed for:

  • Foreign policy analysts
  • Academic researchers
  • Journalists
  • Students of international relations
  • Anyone interested in geopolitics

3. What problem does this solve?

Important diplomatic signals are often buried in:

  • Foreign languages
  • Long speeches
  • Fragmented official sources

We reduce the time and language barriers involved in understanding how leaders frame policy issues over time and across regions.

4. How is this different from traditional news analysis?

Unlike news summaries, this platform:

  • Focuses on primary source material (official speeches)
  • Allows users to conduct their own analysis
  • Emphasizes patterns, trends, and language usage, not editorial interpretation

5. How should this platform be used (and not used)?

This platform is intended as a research and exploratory tool. It is not designed to provide definitive interpretations or policy recommendations. Users are encouraged to combine these tools with contextual knowledge and additional sources.

6. Does the platform include sentiment or intent analysis?

The platform focuses on descriptive text analysis, such as keyword frequency and trend visualization. It does not attempt to infer intent, sentiment, or policy outcomes unless explicitly stated.

7. Where does the data come from?

All content is sourced from publicly available official government websites, press releases, and transcripts. We do not scrape behind paywalls or restricted databases.

8. Do you translate the speeches?

No. We use official English translations published by government sources when they are available.

9. How often is the data updated?

Updates depend on source availability.

10. What are the limitations of the data?

Key limitations include incomplete archival availability, reliance on official translations that may differ in style or emphasis, and structural differences in political communication across systems and time periods. Quantitative analysis cannot fully substitute for contextual interpretation.

Yes. We do not endorse political positions or policy views. The platform focuses on descriptive analysis of language, not normative judgment.

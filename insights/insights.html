Insights

About This Dataset

This tool analyzes speeches from China and Russia to track how geopolitical keywords and themes evolve over time. The dataset includes official speeches from Chinese government representatives (2005-2025) and Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs statements (2014-2025). By visualizing keyword frequency relative to total speech volume, you can identify how great powers prioritize and discuss key geopolitical issues in response to global events.

Example Explorations

Regional Tensions

Try selecting: Taiwan, Hong Kong, Ukraine, and Strategic Stability

Track how frequently great powers discuss contested territories and strategic concerns. Notice how mentions spike during specific crises (2019 Hong Kong protests, 2021 Taiwan tensions, 2022 Ukraine invasion). This reveals how international incidents reshape diplomatic discourse.

Institutional Focus

Try selecting: UN, ASEAN, Shanghai Cooperation Organization

Compare how China and Russia reference different international bodies. Russia's emphasis on the SCO versus China's focus on regional multilateralism reflects distinct strategic priorities and alliance-building approaches.

Geographic Scope

Try selecting: Asia, Europe, Americas (continents)

Analyze which regions dominate great power discourse. The relative frequency of regional mentions indicates where each country concentrates its diplomatic attention and where strategic competition is most intense.

Rhetorical Patterns

Try selecting: Sovereignty, Multipolarity, Rules-based order

Explore how each country frames its policy positions through key rhetorical concepts. Rising frequency of certain terms reflects shifts in how powers justify their actions and attempt to shape international narratives.
